ABA stands for Applied Behavior Analysis. It is the most researched and widely recommended therapy for children with autism spectrum disorder — recognized by pediatricians, school districts, and insurance companies alike.

The Science

How ABA Therapy Works

ABA is built on one central idea: behavior that is rewarded is more likely to happen again. A Board Certified Behavior Analyst (BCBA) identifies which skills your child needs to build — and which behaviors are making daily life harder — then designs a treatment plan specific to your child. No two plans look the same.

Every session follows the ABC framework — a structured way to understand why a behavior is happening and what will change it.

A

Antecedent

What happens just before a behavior. A verbal request, a specific toy, a sound in the room. Understanding the trigger is the first step to changing the response.

B

Behavior

The action being targeted — a skill the team is building, or a behavior they are working to reduce. Defined in clear, measurable terms so progress can be tracked.

C

Consequence

What happens right after. Desired behaviors get immediate, meaningful reinforcement. Behaviors being reduced receive no reaction — never punishment.

Why it works

Consistent reinforcement is essential. Children make faster progress when every session, every therapist, and every caregiver follow the same plan. That is why Intercare trains parents and caregivers as active partners from day one.

Day to Day

What an ABA Session Looks Like

Sessions happen wherever works best for your child — our center, your home, or school. A trained interventionist works directly with your child on their individual goals while a BCBA supervises and adjusts the program.

Sessions are structured but not rigid. They include direct instruction, play-based learning, and real-world practice — because a skill learned in a therapy room only matters if it works at the dinner table, on the playground, and in the classroom.

Parents and caregivers are an active part of the process. Your team will teach you what to do between sessions so your child can practice skills throughout the day, not just during therapy hours. Research shows children make faster progress when families are involved.

Assessment

How Your Child's Treatment Plan Is Built

Every Intercare ABA program starts with a thorough evaluation. Your child's BCBA observes them directly, reviews developmental history, and uses standardized assessments to identify strengths and areas for growth across five domains:

  • Communication
  • Socialization
  • Independent living skills
  • Challenging behaviors
  • Community integration

Once the evaluation is complete, your BCBA works with you to set priorities. Goals are written in measurable terms so you can see exactly how your child is progressing — and the plan changes whenever your child does.

Eligibility

Who Is ABA Therapy For?

ABA therapy is for children and adolescents with autism spectrum disorder. Intercare accepts referrals from 18 months through adolescence.

Early intervention — typically before age six — has the strongest research base. The younger a child starts, the more time they have to build the foundational skills that carry them through school and into adult life.

That said, ABA therapy produces meaningful results at any age. Older children and teens benefit from programs focused on independence, social skills, emotional regulation, and academic support.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Is ABA therapy covered by insurance?

In California, yes. State law (SB 946) requires most commercial insurance plans to cover ABA therapy as a medical benefit. Intercare is in-network with Anthem Blue Cross, Blue Shield of California, Beacon Health Options, Catalight, Molina Healthcare, Optum, Uprise Health, and Magellan Health. Our intake team verifies your insurance before your child's first appointment.

How many hours of ABA therapy does my child need?

It depends on your child's evaluation results, goals, and current skill levels. ABA research supports a wide range of intensities. Your child's BCBA will recommend the right number of hours after the initial evaluation and walk you through the reasoning.

How long does ABA therapy last?

Most children participate for one to three years, though this varies. Your child's team reviews progress on a regular schedule and adjusts both the goals and the intensity of services over time. The aim is always to build independence — not long-term dependence on therapy.

Can ABA therapy happen at my child's school?

Yes. Intercare's BCBAs can attend IEP meetings, observe classrooms, and coordinate directly with your child's school team. Intercare holds a direct service contract with LAUSD to deliver school-based therapy on district campuses, and collaborates with other districts on a case-by-case basis.

What is the difference between a BCBA and an interventionist?

A BCBA (Board Certified Behavior Analyst) designs your child's treatment plan, supervises all services, and makes every clinical decision. An interventionist — also called a behavior technician or Registered Behavior Technician (RBT) — delivers the day-to-day therapy under the BCBA's direct supervision. Intercare requires BCBA oversight for all programs.
